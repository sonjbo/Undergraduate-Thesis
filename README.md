## **Study Overview**
This project examined cultural and socioeconomic influences on schizophrenia prodromal symptoms and stigma across international populations.

A cross-sectional online survey was administered to **822 participants** from **Brazil, India, Italy, and the United States** using Amazon Mechanical Turk. After quality filtering, **200 participants** remained (**121 males**, **79 females**).

Prodromal symptoms were measured using the **Prodromal Questionnaireâ€“Brief (PQ-B)**, capturing both symptom presence and associated distress. Attitudes toward schizophrenia were assessed using the **Stereotypes of Schizophrenia Scale**.

**Primary hypothesis:**  
Lower income economies would show higher rates of prodromal symptoms.

Predictive associations were examined between prodromal symptoms and:
- attitudes toward schizophrenia  
- immigration status  
- substance/cannabis use  
- income  
- gender  
- age
